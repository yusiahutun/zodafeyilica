A phenomenon known as the planning fallacy occurs when one who estimates the amount of time required to do a task in the future, exhibits optimism bias. The term planning fallacy describes a prediction phenomenon that many people are all too acquainted with: people underestimate how long a task will take to complete in the future, even when they are aware that such activities have typically taken longer than anticipated in the past.
