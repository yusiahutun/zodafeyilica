    Hair oil is one of the usual sources of nourishment that tends to prevent loss of moisture from the hair follicles – it maintains the water level within the keratinous cells of the hair shafts. However, with low-porosity hair, the assimilation of nutrients remains a hindrance hence the implementation of the best oils for low-porosity hair into one's hair care regime is a must. Besides, hair with low porosity has its cuticle layer loosely packed, with small pockets that allow moisture to pass into each strand, preventing dryness. At the same time, this can make your hair super greasy and oily. 

    The hair has several functions apart from amplifying the overall aesthetic appeal of an individual like facilitating osmoregulation and acting as a barrier against external entrants (1). When it comes to maintaining our hair, oil can be a great ally. A good hair oil — grapeseed, argan, marula, jojoba, apricot, pomegranate, Kalahari melon seed, or sweet almond oil with low molecular weight, shields the hair against external pollutants and makes a way for nutrient absorption by elevating existing levels of hair porosity. 

    Lightweight hair oils contain smaller molecules that are easier to penetrate through the rigid shafts of the hair. These molecules tend to ease out the bonds of keratinous proteins, facilitating moisture absorption and accelerating hair growth. If you are struggling with low-porosity hair and wish to find a hair oil that suits you, we’ve mentioned a few recommendations below.

    Low porosity hair can be a real problem as it prevents the entry of moisturizing and nourishing agents into the keratinous cells of the hair strands. The lack of moisture gradually makes the hair frizzy and more susceptible to split ends, thereby leading to breakage. Here are a few factors that you can consider before buying a hair oil for low-porosity hair.

    How to Choose the Best Hair Oil for Low-porosity Hair?

    Choosing the best hair oil for low-porosity hair can be a major challenge as every hair type and scalp is different. We have listed a few general factors that you can take into consideration before making a purchase:

    1. Density and Texture of the Oil: Oils that are used for low-porosity hair should not be too dense as heavy oils have a more compact and rigid structure that does not work well for hair with low porosity. They should also have a pleasant odor so that it doesn't ruin the entire experience of indulging in self-care. At the same time, it should not have an extremely runny texture. 

    2. Structural Composition of Hair Oils: The structural composition of hair oils for low porosity hair should be free from harmful chemical additives, parabens, sulfates, and phthalates – This makes it skin-friendly and does not cause slow damage to the hair and the body in general. It should be composed of natural ingredients and preservative-free to help benefit the user to the maximum. 

    3. A Standard Value of Saponification: The oil procured should have a standard saponification value to promote the smooth and easy removal of thegreasy oil residue during shampooing. It should not be too high as it will make the oil removal extremely difficult or it should not be too low either.

    How to Take Care of Low-porosity Hair?

    Here are a few ways you can take care of low-porosity hair:

    1. Use Light-weight Oils: Using lightweight oils can be really beneficial for low-porosity hair as they will easily be absorbed through the keratin bonds and make way for effective moisturization. 

    2. Steam Your Hair Often: Steaming your hair or indulging in a salon spa once every fortnight can be beneficial for low porosity hair, as has the ability to penetrate through the walls of the follicles and be more readily absorbed than water itself. 

    3. Avoid Protein-rich Products: Applying protein-rich products onto our hair like eggs, and soymilk can further make the keratin cells cling to one another. However, you can moderately include protein in your diet. 

    4. Avoid Styling Products: As hair with low porosity is more compact and dense, it will prevent the absorption of styling products. This will over time tend to cause build-up and gradually lead to issues like breakage and, dandruff.

    How to Apply Oil for Low Porosity Hair?

    It is always recommended to steam your hair before the process of applying oil, but if you want to skip this step you can. However, it is always advisable to steam your at least twice a month. 

    Step 1 - Check whether your oil is an essential oil or a base oil. If it is an essential oil like argan oil or tea tree oil, do mix it with another base oil like coconut or sweet almond oil to enhance dilution.

    Step 2 -  Add a few drops of the essential oil to the base oil. 

    Step 3 - Heat the oil on a medium flame and let it cool for a while.

    Step 4 - Start applying the heated oil from the roots and towards the ends. 

    Step 5 - Let the oil remain on the hair for about thirty minutes. 

    Step 6 - Use lukewarm water to wash off the oil and make sure that the water is not too hot as this can cause breakage.

    Conclusion

    The easiest way to pamper yourself is by indulging in a hot oil massage with the best oils for low-porosity hair. As the oil seeps into your hair follicles, it releases accumulated stress and enhances blood circulation. But with low porosity hair, moisture absorption and retention is a real problem. These problems tend to further lead to issues like frizziness, dandruff, breakage, and rough hair. Hence having hair oils with a lightweight base and a simple composition can be extremely beneficial for low-porosity hair.

    FAQ's

    Why do individuals with low-porosity hair need special oils?

    Individuals with low-porosity hair need lightweight oils like jojoba, marula, apricot, etc. to rejuvenate their scalp and maintain moisture levels.

    Is coconut oil suitable for low-porosity hair?

    Yes, coconut oil is suitable for low-porosity hair, but it must be used in moderation.

    Can olive oil be used for low-porosity hair?

    Yes, olive oil can be used for low-porosity hair to reap maximum moisturization benefits.

    What are the benefits of using jojoba oil for low-porosity hair?

    Jojoba oil penetrates the hair shaft to nourish and rejuvenate your hair and scalp from within.

    How often should oils be applied to low-porosity hair?

    You should apply oils on low-porosity hair 2-3 times a week.

    Are there specific oils to avoid for low-porosity hair?

    Heavy oils like coconut and olive oil must be avoided or used in moderation on low-porosity hair.

    Can oil blends be effective for low-porosity hair?

    No, it's not advised to use oil blends on low-porosity hair, as they can weigh down your mane, making it greasy and oily.
