    Early Life

    Michael Dapaah was born on the 10th of August 1991, in London, England.

    Before his mainstream success as a comedian, Dapaah was working in Vodafone; he said that he’d had enough of that life, and wanted to focus on something that allowed him to unleash more creativity.

    This is when he began creating comedy skits and creating characters like Big Shaq, who’s now become a huge sensation online.

    Career

    Early Acting Career

    Michael Dapaah’s upbringing in Croydon, as part of a diverse, Ghanaian immigrant family, played a significant role in his choice of career.

    His multicultural upbringing in South London and his parent’s desire for him to become a doctor were pulling him in opposite directions.

    Dapaah’s interests leaned more toward the arts, specifically acting and comedy, a path he hoped would lead to financial success and fame.

    After spending time in South Africa as a child and following a period of incarceration, Michael’s perspective on life changed.

    In pursuit of his dream, he attended Brunel University, studying for his arts degree and honing his skills in film, acting, and theatre.

    Brunel University produced its fair share of famous, successful celebrity graduates, including actors, singers, and other luminaries such as Greg Davies.

    Dapaah’s commitment to acting led him to enroll in courses at the National Youth Theatre to further develop his acting abilities.

    Success at the National Youth Theatre

    After his early success at the National Youth Theatre, Michael gained a following in the British-African community for his comedic roles, establishing a strong fanbase.

    He made brief comedic appearances in the television show, “Meet the Adebanjos,” which centered around a Nigerian family in London, showcasing his comedic talent.

    The show hit a chord as its focus on instilling old-fashioned African values by a flamboyant and outspoken father harked back to his own family.

    His performance was well-received and later led to the launch of “SWIL” (Somewhere in London) together with fellow Brunel graduate, Marv Brown.

    The collaboration between these eager young actors launched the mockumentary series “SWIL” on YouTube, signaling a significant step in his career.

    Becoming a YouTube Sensation

    Seeing an opportunity on YouTube and realizing that he could take more direct control of his future, Michael threw himself into the new medium.

    But his big break came with his appearance on “Fire in the Booth” with Charlie Sloth, BBC’s Radio 1Xtra show that catapulted him to fame.

    In the show, Dapaah produced a comedy skit in which he parodied UK gangster stereotypes and rubbished rappers, becoming an “overnight” success.

    Comedian

    He went viral on YouTube with his “Fire in the Booth” freestyle clip, performing as “Roadman Shaq,” a character all too recognizable at the time.

    This was followed by another viral success, a comedic rap called “Man’s Not Hot,” which resonated with his global audience and sparked a meme craze.

    Other famous rap celebrities like Stormzy have used their time on the BBC 1XTRA show to help boost their YouTube views and revenue as well.

    Musician

    The official release of his single saw Dapaah’s position in the music industry strengthen, attracting yet more attention both online and for live events.

    With Michael’s career taking off and seeing the success of his latest rap song, he produced a studio track version of “Man’s Not Hot”.

    The “Man’s Not Hot” music video featured cameos by artists Waka Flocka Flame, DJ Khaled, and Dutch rap group Broederliefde, further elevating Dapaah’s profile.

    Health and Fitness Influencer

    In an unexpected move, Dapaah ventured into the fitness industry in 2020, creating “Belly Must Go,” showcasing his versatility in content creation.

    Rather than focussing on his comedic talent, Michael created a serious health program with a range of topics covering both fitness and nutrition.

    A notable episode included an interview with Francis Ngannou, with the MMA fighter and UFC heavyweight champion showing Michael what it’s like in the ring.

    Michael Dapaah’s career took a creative turn, marked by his diverse array of talents, including acting, comedy, music, and health and fitness.

    International Recognition

    This resulted in further international recognition, with followers worldwide appreciating his creative abilities as a comic, songwriter, and lifestyle influencer.

    Such is his influence, that his character, Big Shaq, and the catchphrase “Man’s Not Hot” became iconic in popular culture, helping to boost his popularity.

    With an active social media presence and close connection with fans, his creative projects are sure to see his income steadily increasing in the future.
