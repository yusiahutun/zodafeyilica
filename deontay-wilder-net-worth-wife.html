    Career

    • Deontay Wilder started his amateur boxing career when he joined Skyy Boxing Gym in Northport, Alabama in October 2005.
    • He entered the National Golden Gloves competition in the year 2007 where he defeated David Thompson and the world champion, Isiah Thomas.
    • In the following year, he participated in the US Amateur Boxing Championship and defeated Quantis Graves.
    • He recorded his career-best score by defeating Rakhim Chakhiyev from Russia in the year 2008.
    • Deontay made his debut in professional boxing, at the Vanderbilt University Memorial Gymnasium on 15th November 2008.
    • He won his first professional boxing title, by defeating Kelvin Price at the Memorial Sports Arena in Los Angeles on 15th December 2012.
    • In April 2013, the boxing firm Golden Boy Promotions announced a fight between Deontay and Audley Harrison a former Olympic gold medalist.
    • A fight was announced between Deontay and American boxer Malik Scott in February 2014.
    • After the fight, he secured third place, and Scott was placed at number 23, in the world rankings.
    • After defeating Malik, he took over as the mandatory challenger for the WBC heavyweight title.
    • Deontay fought the defending champion, Bermane Stiverne, at the MGM Grand Garden Arena in Las Vegas on 17th January 2015.
    • In May 2015, he fought his first defense of the WBC heavyweight title in Alabama.
    • He fought his second defense, against Johann Duhaupas, ranked number 12 in the WBC in August 2015.
    • Deontay defended his title against Polish boxer Artur Szpilka in December 2015.
    • In July 2016, he fought Chris Arreola who is a former two-time world title challenger.
    • The WBC ordered Wilder to fight the mandatory challenger, Bermane Stiverne in October 2017.
    • On 18th May 2019, he defended WBC heavyweight title against Dominic Breazeale via knockout in the first round.
    • Further, he caught him with a powerful right which sent Breazeale flat on his back and unable to continue with the referee waving off the contest after reaching the ten count.
