    The Hollywood Renaissance, which happened in the mid-1960 to early 1980's saw the emergence of talented young filmmakers from different races. The iconic black actors from the 60s and 70s helped redefine the entertainment world. They challenged the stereotypes placed on them and influenced the types of films produced, their marketing, and their production.

    Hollywood is a big industry that provides work to hundreds of actors. Carving out a name in Hollywood needs extraordinary talent, persistence, and time.

    At the advent of this film industry, it was mostly dominated by white actors. But black actors from the 60s and 70s were spotlighted because of their unmatched acting talents. They changed the game for the new black generation of Hollywood hopefuls.

    Famous black actors from the 60s and 70s

    The world has witnessed many talented black actors, and it is hard to choose the best. According to Essence, these are the ten famous black male actors from the 60s and 70s. They have won numerous prestigious awards and nominations for their incredible work.

    1. Morgan Freeman

    Who was a famous black actor from the 60s? Morgan Freeman (born on 1st June 1937) is one of the most famous black actors from 1960s.

    Morgan started professional acting in the 1960s. In 1964 he featured in The Royal Hunt of the Sun and appeared as an extra in a drama film The Pawnbroker.

    Freeman acted in several movies in the 60s, and he perfected his art throughout the 70s, winning the Clarence Derwent Awards, Drama Desk, Tony Awards, and more. However, he got his major Hollywood breakthrough in the 80s.

    Besides film, Morgan Freeman works as a voiceover artist, having features in several productions.

    2. Samuel Leroy Jackson

    Samuel Jackson (born on 21st December 1948) is an actor and producer. He is one of the most famous black actors of his generation.

    What black celebrities were popular in the 70s? There were several black celebrities in the '70s, but Samuel Jackson stood out. Jackson began his acting career onstage with A Soldiers Play and Home. He appeared in several TV films, and in 1972, he made his feature film debut in Together for Days.

    3. James Earl Jones

    James Earl Jones (born on 17th January 1931) is one the most versatile and distinguished black actors. He has been in the industry for over six decades, since 1952, but he is still active in acting. Jones has starred in several films, but the notable ones are The Lion King, Best Man, Coming to America, and Conan the Barbarian.

    4. Sidney Poitier

    Sidney Poitier (20th February 1927 – 6th January 2022) was a diplomat, film director, and actor. In 1964, Poitier was the first Bahamian and black actor to win the prestigious Academy Award for Best Actor. He also received a BAFTA Award, two Golden Globe Awards, a Grammy Award, as well as a nomination for a Tony Award and two Emmy Awards.

    Some of the films that Poitier starred in or was featured in include Lilies of the Field, A Raisin in the Sun, Blackboard Jungle, and Porgy and Bess.

    5. Billy Dee Williams

    Billy Dee Williams (born on 6th April 1937) is an actor and painter. His first film debut was in 1959 in The Last Angry Man. But he became famous for his role in the movie Brian's Song (1971); it earned him an Emmy nomination for Best Actor.

    6. Danny Glover

    Danny Glover (born on 22nd July 1946) is a film director, actor, and political activist. He is famous for his star roles in Lethal Weapon, Predator 2, Angels in the Outfield, To Sleep with Anger, Operation Dumbo, etc.

    Glover has won several accolades, including NAACP's President Award and Jean Hersholt Humanitarian Award. In addition, he got nominations for four Grammy Awards, a Daytime Emmy Award, and four Primetime Emmy Awards. In 2023, Danny was inducted into the prestigious Black Music and Entertainment Walk of Fame.

    7. Ernie Hudson

    Earnest Hudson (born on 17th December 1945) is one of the famous black actors from the 60s and 70s. He is best known for his roles in Ghostbusters, The Ron Clark Story, Airheads, Miss Congeniality, and more.

    Hudson started his film career as the resident playwright at Detroit's Concept East, the oldest black theatre in America. After his debut in the 1976 film Leadbelly, Ernie has featured in over 100 TV shows and about 100 movies.

    8. Gregory Oliver Hines

    Gregory Hines (14th February 1946 – 9th August 2003) was a choreographer, dancer, singer, and actor. He is among the most celebrated tap dancer of all time. The performer successfully transitioned from stage to music, perfectly merging dance and acting in his films.

    He is best known in television for his roles in Little Bill, Will and Grace, The Gregory Hines Show, The Cotton Club, and The Wolfen. Oliver starred in over 40 films and received many accolades, including a Drama Desk Award, Daytime Emmy Award, and Tony Award. He also got nominations for four Primetime Emmy Awards and Screen Actors Guild Awards.

    9. Harry Belafonte

    Harry Belafonte (1st March 1927 – 25th April 2023) was an activist and actor who popularized calypso music in the 1950s. Belafonte is one of the most famous performers to have received a Tony (EGOT), Oscar, Grammy, and Emmy awards. However, he won the Oscar award in a non-competitive category.

    Besides music, Harry starred in numerous films, including Bright Road, Island in the Sun, The World the Flesh and The Devil.

    10. Ossie Davis

    Ossie Davis (18th December 1917 – 4th February 2005) was an activist, writer, director, and actor. Ossie had a very successful career spanning over five decades.

    He is one of the most awarded artists of his time. Davis has a Grammy, an Emmy, and several NAACP Image Awards. He also shares the National Medal of Arts with his wife and actress, Ruby Dee.
