    Dancing On Ice is back, with the well-loved show set to arrive on the nation's screens for its 14th series and a whole new lineup of contestants.

    With the magic of Christmas behind us and nothing but gloomy days and early nights ahead for the next month or so, what better way is there to spend your January evenings than tucked up in front of the winter wonderland that is Dancing On Ice?

    Other than a three-year break, the show has run since 2006 and so is a bonafide regular in the prime time slot.

    For most of that time, national treasures Holly Willoughby and Phillip Schofield have been the faces of the show.

    The pair first started hosting in 2006, carrying on together as a pair until 2011 when Holly left. Phillip carried on for three more years until 2014 but then he too left the show.

    In the meantime, Christine Lampard took over as the show's host. In 2018, Holly and Phil made their glorious return and have been holding court ever since.

    The show is built in a specially constructed rink in RAF Bovingdon near Hemel Hempstead in Hertfordshire.

    It's been filmed here ever since it returned to our screens in 2018.

    The return came after a three-year break, before which it had been shot at the George Lucas Stage at Elstree Studios in Hertfordshire.

    In the past, speaking about the new set, Phil said: “Yes it has ice in the centre of it and there is a competition but it will look and feel different. There is a new studio, Jayne and Chris are on the panel and that frees up our professional dancers to choreograph.”

    Getting tickets to Dancing On Ice is possible, but that doesn't mean it's easy.

    SRO audiences are tasked with the allocation of tickets. They say about their ticket policy: “We try to be as fair as we can about issuing the tickets. Successful applicants are randomly selected from amongst those who apply. We aim to email tickets to the successful applicants as quickly as possible.”

    However, the already-difficult task has become even harder during the pandemic. SRO says: “This year for Dancing On Ice, we shall be admitting the audience in a different way to usual; we never know in advance whether each group will attend, or will use all of their tickets, and we therefore over-issue tickets to compensate for the inevitable no-shows and base the over-issue on the attendance at previous recordings of a show.

    “At most shows, we admit regular ticket holders on a first-come first-served basis. We shall be recording this show with each group will be seated at a table.

    “In order to make the most of the available space and accommodate as many people as possible, groups who fit the available tables will be favoured.

    “Therefore, although hopefully we shall still accommodate everyone who attends, at this production, if a group arrives after your party but the size of their group fits the available seats and yours does not, that group may be admitted first.”

    When is Dancing on Ice back?

    The show will return on Sunday, January 16, 2022 at 6:30pm on ITV.
