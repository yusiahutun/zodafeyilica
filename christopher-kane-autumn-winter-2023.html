    This season, we are breaking down the autumn/winter 2023 collections with a new franchise, The Fashion Week Cheat Sheet. After speaking to designers about their inspiration, their hero pieces, the faces on the catwalk and the names on the front row, we present everything you need to know about AW23.

    “My customer wants something that no one else has,” Christopher Kane told me this week, ahead of presenting his autumn/winter 2023 collection at a no-frills venue in Angel, where models wore clothes that were inspired by the working-class environment that he grew up in. “Memories of outfits worn day to night by his mother, aunts, and neighbours in the 1980s, the collection is a celebration of the complexities of growing up in a working-class environment,” his show notes read. “Housewives, cleaners, barmaids all play a significant role.”

    Latex skirts and dresses with statement peplum hems, enormous collars, vibrant primary colours and animal-printed dresses all came down the catwalk, nodding to the fashion Kane grew up around, but also delivering something entirely unique and unexpected for his customer.

    Giovanni Giannoni//Getty Images

    Theme and inspiration

    “Inspiration can come from the oddest of places and each season is different,” Kane says. “One time I went to the dentist and found clips that inspired an entire collection. Most of the time it comes directly from my sketches and toiles. I have learnt over the years to be open and not to put too much pressure on finding something, it is part of the process, and it always comes good. In fact, for AW23 I am still deciding what influenced some of my decisions.”

    “With every collection, it’s a combination of sketching, playing around on the body and figuring out what the feeling is, the latter is instinctive,” he continues. “The colour comes later – once I’ve concluded the silhouette and overall feeling. It’s the only way I can describe it really – the process is all about experimenting and having an open mind each season.”

    For autumn/winter 2023, the key silhouette was “the simple geometric forms that surround the head, shoulders and hips” – he presented dresses with chopping board collars that both frame and conceal the face, while he used bustles on the front and back of the body, reminiscent of tied bin bags and barmaid-style skirts “that create a sultry waddle”.

    Colour-wise, it was all about school uniform charcoals with pops of primary colours, but it was the prints which probably stole the show. Several tops and dresses featured AI-generated animal prints of rats, butterflies, pigs and chicks. They were certainly unexpected – Kane said were there to “create a modern wardrobe for those who desire clothes that challenge sartorial tastes and standards”.

    When it came to the show setting, Kane wanted the focus to be entirely on the clothes, something he says he learnt from putting on a much larger spectacle in September. “For SS23 we went big at the Camden Roundhouse, it was a great, beautiful show but I realised on the day of the show it felt somewhat impersonal. For AW23 I wanted to return to a simpler space – the venue itself has no frills. I don’t do tricks – my focus is always to make innovative original collections.”

    The make-up was just as pared-back. “We wanted the skin to look super glossy, almost shiny,” Sofia Tilbury told us.

    Hero pieces

    Those which featured that unique, statement silhouette. “It’s a feeling,” he says on why he created those unexpected shapes. “Like when you get close to the sublime, there isn’t anywhere else to go. I feel the same way with these pieces – there isn’t any other way to articulate it. They are the perfect balance of portraying femininity with the sharp square lines relating to realities of life.”

    “It’s so funny, as when I’m saying this I think of the saying, ‘She’s got a chip on her shoulder’; this goes beyond that but more of a crutch than a hindrance. I also just love the simplicity of framing heads.”

    Who was there?

    Alexa Chung, Poppy Delevingne, Richie Shazam, Clara Amfo, Pip Millett, Nikki Lilly and Reece Clarke were among the names to sit on the front row.

    The message

    “I don’t know if I can have a proper objective opinion [about the mood of fashion today] as I’m so close to it. What I will say is that it is a very different environment to when I started my business in 2006. Social media has changed the way the fashion industry operates, it has its pros and cons, it is an exciting time to work in the industry. I feel it’s opening more to become more diverse and less exclusive, which is a great and necessary change.”

    But, although the industry is certainly different to when Christopher Kane launched, what his customer wants has not changed. They desire something that pushes the boundaries – and he knows exactly how to deliver this. “I want my clothes to have a superpower quality about them; whether it’s to command attention at the workplace, seduce, or quietly disrupt a cocktail or evening event. My designs are forever pieces.”
