    Nairobi pastor slams critics of Christmas celebrations

    - Godfrey Migwi said Christians should celebrate events and mystery not the date - He cited pentecost which was an agricultural feast but is today synonymous with Christianity - He called out the critics for their hypocrisy for observing end year cross over vigils and not Christmas Day

    25 Mar 2024, 00:00

    - Godfrey Migwi said Christians should celebrate events and mystery not the date

    - He cited pentecost which was an agricultural feast but is today synonymous with Christianity

    - He called out the critics for their hypocrisy for observing end year cross over vigils and not Christmas Day

    Nairobi televangelist Godfrey Migwi has faulted churches and Christians who neither celebrate nor recognise Christmas Day.

    A debate has always arisen during the day's celebrations on when and where exactly the word Christmas is mentioned in the Bible as the day Jesus was born and that it should be remembered.

    However, according to Pastor Migwi, the discussion around Jesus' exact date of birth is neither here not there since it is the events and the mystery recorded in scripture not the date that is important.

    "First thereâ€™s funny mentality of those who confuse terminology with theology. Easter as a term is not in the Bible but we celebrate Easter because it's an event and a mystery recorded in scripture. Same with Christmas, it's not the term or calendar date we celebrate but the event and mystery," he pointed out.

    The man of God further cited the term pentecost which is now synonymous with Pentecostal Church was an agricultural feast that had nothing to do with the Holy Spirit.

    Just like SDA faithful, members of the Pentecostal Church do not commemmorate Jesus' birthday which other Christians believe happened on December 25.

    "A Pentecostal church that says it doesn't celebrate Christmas because the term doesn't exist in the Bible but calls itself a Pentecostal church is just a comic contradiction. Pentecost means 50th day, it was an agricultural feast that had nothing to do with the Holy Spirit until that particular year that the Holy Spirit fell on the apostles. Even after that there is no record in scripture that the Holy Spirit fell on other people on Pentecost day," he explained.

    That said, the preacher decried hypocrisy in the church wondering why the same members often observe cross over vigils which are not in the Bible.

    "Traditional marriage, village meetings, cross over vigils, show me where it's in the Bible' . Folks who are addicted to 31st Dec Cross over vigil don't even know it's not in the Bible... Finally, this nonsense must stop this year of 2020," he noted.
