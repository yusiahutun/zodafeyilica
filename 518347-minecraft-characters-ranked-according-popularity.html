    Minecraft characters

    Minecraft characters come in different shapes, colours and sizes. You may need clarification on whom to choose and their roles. According to Blogging.org, these are the most popular characters worth watching.

    1. The Ender Dragon

    The Ender Dragon is a powerful and iconic boss mob in Minecraft's sandbox video game. It is among the most popular Minecraft characters in the game world. You will find the Dragon in the End dimension, a unique and challenging area players can access by entering an End Portal located in strongholds.

    Ender Dragon has massive wings, a breath of fire, and laser eyes that terrorise its enemies. It is not easily defeated, yet satisfaction comes when the beast is down. Apart from fiery wings, you can identify the Dragon with the obsidian pillars and healing crystals surrounding it.

    2. Steve

    If you are a Minecraft player, you must be familiar with Steve. He is one of the main Minecraft characters in the game. He is among the default players that new Minecraft gamers use at the beginning of the game. Steve is known for his blocky appearance, consisting of a simple body of cubes, which aligns with the game's pixelated aesthetic.

    3. Alex

    Alex is among the popular female Minecraft characters in the sandbox video game Minecraft. Just like Steve, Alex is the default player in the game. She provides players with the female option in the game; therefore, you are likely to meet her at the beginning, making her popular.

    Unlike Steve, she has slightly thinner arms and long hair, giving her a more feminine look. Her default clothing comprises a teal shirt, brown pants, and grey shoes. You can customise her as a player to give you your preferred look.

    4. Creeper

    The Creeper always features in the list of Minecraft characters. They have distinct pale green colours all over the body, resembling vines or leaves. Creepers have a cylindrical torso with short legs and arms. They are also a hostile mob in the famous Minecraft game. Creepers are well known for speaking up on a player right before they explode.

    5. Zombie Pigman

    Zombie Pigmen were initially passive mobs, meaning they wouldn't attack players unless provoked. They were replaced by Zombified Piglins in the game, leading to their extinction. Zombie Pigmen can live in other species, such as Magma cubes, Ghasts and Winter Skeletons.

    These characters can be easily identified by the sword in their right hand as well as their loincloth. They were also a combination of a zombie and a pig, having the body shape of a pig but the skin and behaviour of a zombie.

    6. Villager

    The Villager is a famous character in Minecraft games. Gamers get to interact with them when they desire to trade supplies. In Minecraft, villagers work several jobs, each offering something distinctively different from the previous one. They will immediately move adjacent to their job block as soon as the sun rises.

    It's important to understand what profession each does, even though some may be more beneficial than others, depending on what you're looking for. You can always modify a villager's job if you're unsatisfied with it. They also have iron golems as protectors, attacking hostile mobs approaching the villagers.

    7. Herobrine

    The Herobrine is one of the popular Minecraft characters that gained popularity for being a Creepypasta. Many players attest that Herobrine still haunts them in their game world for appearing without an alarm with a disturbing presence. He is also known for his manipulative skill and mysterious ways in the game.

    The Herobrine legend emerged as a hoax, a form of an online horror story or urban legend. The myth suggests that Herobrine is a mysterious, powerful, and often evil figure resembling the default player, Steve, but has white, empty eyes.

    8. Wither Storm

    The Wither Storm is a powerful and destructive creature that serves as the main antagonist in the first season of Minecraft: Story Mode. Wither Storm is known for its imposing presence and the challenges it presents to other characters. It has a command block within its chest, three wither skeleton skulls and soul sand.

    9. Enderman

    Endermen typically don't pay attention to players but run to fight those who hurt or stare them in the face. Endermen occasionally pick up specific blocks and use teleportation to avoid being hit by projectiles, sunlight, water (which is harmful to them), and other kinds of damage.

    10. Skeletons

    Skeletons are aggressive, hostile mobs found in two dimensions and are known to drop expensive loot when killed. They are famous characters in Minecraft that often become more active at night.

    They are also characterised by their bony appearance and long-range attacks using bows and arrows. You can see them spawning in dimly lit or dark areas like caves and dungeons. Skeletons are crucial to the Minecraft game, providing players with resources during the game.

    11. Wolf

    A wolf is a neutral mob. Most hostile mobs can be defeated with the help of a domesticated wolf. They are known for their loyalty, protective behaviour, and ability to assist players in various ways. You can tame wolves using bones from Skeletons after they are defeated.

    It is not easy to tame a wolf. However, this Minecraft character is useful in combat if well-tamed. Tamed wolves will automatically rush to defend their owner when hostile mobs attack the player. They cause damage by biting and can help fend off enemies.

    12. Ghast

    Ghasts are enormous, vicious, ghost-like floating white creatures who inhabit the Nether and unleash exploding fireballs at players. You can identify a Ghast by their distinctive wailing sounds and ability to shoot explosive fireballs at the player. Generally, they have a ghostly and intimidating look that scares their opponents or enemies.

    14. Magma Cubes

    In the Nether, Magma cubes can be found in all lighting conditions. They must be able to spawn in an area free of solid obstacles and liquids. Magma Cubes can sprout atop magma blocks since they are essentially fireproof. They are unique in appearance and behaviour, offering players a different challenge than other mobs in Minecraft.

    15. Ocelots and cats

    Ocelots are passive mobs that can be tamed into cats using raw fish. Even though the two have many similarities, you can identify them by their colours. Cats are smaller than Ocelots and come in different breeds and colours. You can identify Ocelots by their spotted textures and larger sizes.

    16. Phantoms

    Phantoms are flying hostile mobs that appear if a player hasn't slept for several in-game days. They are known for their high-pitched screeching sounds. They were introduced to the game in the 1.13 Update Aquatic. They spawn at night on players who have not slept for some time, damaging them.
