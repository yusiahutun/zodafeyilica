    How can I prevent ingrown hairs when trimming my bikini area?

    According to experts, since the hair around your vulva is coarser and curlier than the rest of the hair on your body, it's more susceptible to ingrown hairs. Exfoliating the area with a chemical exfoliant like lactic or salicylic acid to get rid of dead skin cells before reaching for your device is a key way to avoid potential ingrowns. Additionally, trimming in the direction of your hair growth and avoiding the closest setting can be helpful.

    What's the easiest way to decide which trimmer is right for me?

    The best bikini trimmers can come with a host of attachments and size options, but ultimately, the best option for you depends on your hair removal preferences. Opt for a pen-sized trimmer for quick touch-ups, or to target your bikini line exclusively, or one with a wider head to remove larger areas of hair at once.
