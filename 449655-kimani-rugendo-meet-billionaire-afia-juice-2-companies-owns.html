    Kimani Rugendo transitioned from politics to business after the 1992 polls where he lost the Lang'ata parliamentary seat to Raila Odinga.

    The former Ford-Asili party Nairobi chairman established Kevian Kenya, a company that produced Mt Kenya bottled water.

    Rugendo began serious production of the product in 1995 with KSh 25 million from savings and friends.

    Pick ‘N’ Peel and Afia

    Kevian Kenya later diversified its range of products by launching the Pick ‘N’ Peel and Afia drink juices.

    The products are being sold across the Eastern African region, including Sudan and Zambia.

    Second factory in Thika

    As demand for the products increased, Rugendo built a second factory in Thika in 2005.

    The company increased its production from 5,000 litres per hour to 20,000 for its Pick ‘N’ Peel juice.

    On the other hand, Afia's capacity rose from 10,000 bottles hourly to 40,000.

    Besides juices, Kevian also produces malt non-alcoholic drinks. These include Plain Malt, Energy Malt, Lemon Malt and Orange Malt.

    Kevian's KSh 2 billion investment

    In June 2016, the company invested KSh 2 billion to manufacture other products such as coffee, vegetable soups and tomato sauce.

    The products are sold at different Kenyan supermarkets targeting the middle-class and high-end hotels.

    Sterling Kenya

    Rugendo at the same time used KSh 500 million and registered a new company - Sterling Kenya to produce water tanks.

    According to its website, the subsidiary manufactures cylindrical, underground and loft water tanks with capacities of between 1000 to 11,000 litres.

    "SterlingCraft Tanks are made from 100% virgin polyethene material, meaning it is made from brand new powder making it superior to other tanks manufactured using regrind material," it stated.

    Sterling Craft Limited

    Sterling Craft Limited industrial equipment and military regalia.

    Rugendo founded it in the early 1980s to supply items such as berets, ceremonial swords and medallions to the Kenya Police Service and Kenya Defense Forces (KDF).

    Besides producing military regalia, the company also sells pipes, fittings, generators and milking machines.

    Kevian potato production

    In July 2020, Business Daily reported that the juice maker ventured into the potato production business after inking a deal with the Kenya Agriculture and Livestock Research Organisation (KALRO).

    KALRO awarded Kevian a 15-year contract to produce potato tubers targeting 50,000 farmers.

    Philip Ndegwa's wealth

    In related news, Central Bank of Kenya governor Philip Ndegwa who died on January 7, 1996, left behind the multi-billion wealth he had accumulated for over 30 years.
