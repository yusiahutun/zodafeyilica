    Pre-holiday beauty routine How to get bikini-ready

    Get completely holiday-ready in just one month with our countdown guide to all things summer beauty.

    Four weeks to go

    Cleanse and detoxify: "Start taking artichoke extract (10 drops in 500ml of water) in the morning to wash out toxins and ease the puffiness caused by water retention," says the nutritionist Vicki Edgson. Swapping your latte for a smoothie with maca powder will also help to reduce bloating. Sweating is another way to detoxify your body: "Three body workouts a week combined with five minutes in the sauna will help create a lean body," says the fitness expert Lee Mullins.

    Circulation booster: If you suffer with puffy ankles, boost the lymphatic flow by "using a tennis ball to massage the arch of your foot while sitting at your desk or watching the TV," says the body expert Martine de Richeville. Learn how to reduce swelling around your eyes and jawline with a FaceGym Signature Xpress Workout, £20 for 15 minutes at Selfridges.

    Block book treatments: One full-body treatment each week will have your skin smoothed. Four Clarins TriActive the Body Lift Sculptors, £67 for 85 minutes, will leave you feeling exfoliated and moisturised from head to toe, while a Carita Lift Expert Body treatment once a week, £65 for 45 minutes, sculpts your thighs, bottom and stomach.

    Emma Tempest for Harper's Bazaar

    Three weeks to go

    Hydrate and exfoliate: Body brushing is still one of the most effective ways to tone and smooth your thighs and bottom. Ideally, you should body brush all year round, but three weeks is enough time to see results. Use a Mio Body Brush, £14.50, every other day before you shower. Long, gentle circular movements, starting at your ankles, will help boost circulation. For skin in need of extra hydration, apply body oil – try Jo Malone London Shower Oil, £32 – before you get into the shower. It will remove any leftover self-tan and nourish your skin, to prevent it from feeling stripped or dry after exfoliation. Twice a week, use Balance Me Super Toning Body Polish, £18, or Ole Henriksen Rub n' Buff Salt Scrub, £28, to remove dead skin.

    Firm and tone: Swap your usual body lotions for firming-and-toning formulas. While these won't eradicate cellulite, they strengthen skin to give the appearance of smoother thighs. Bazaar's favourites include Dr Sebagh's Supreme Body, £130, and Radical's Firming Body Multi Repair, £65.

    Trim your locks: 'It's best to have your hair cut when you return from your holiday to get rid of any damage the sun, sea and chlorine might have caused. However, to really look your best while on holiday, get a trim three weeks before,' says the A-list hairstylist George Northwood.

    Two weeks to go

    Time to tan: At this point in the process, your skin should feel soft and smooth. A natural-looking tan will make you feel more toned in a bikini and will disguise any imperfections. For a healthy glow, start with a light gradual tan, such as Lancôme Flash Bronzer Night Sun, £24, for your face, and Bondi Sands Everyday Gradual Tanning Milk, £11.99, St Tropez Gradual Tan, £14.50, or Crème de la Mer the Face and Body Gradual Tan, £75, mixed with an oil-free lotion for your body and applied every other day. "If you shower before you tan, blast your skin with cold water, as this helps to close pores and prevent them from getting blocked," says St Tropez's Tanning Expert Jules Heptonstall.

    Evening ritual: Take a bath two to three times this week with a cup full of Epsom or detoxifying salts, such as Being Float Salt Bath, £12, to reduce any excess water retention – it's a trick used by Gwyneth Paltrow and Victoria Beckham.

    Before bed every evening: "Give your tummy a deep, circular massage," advises Martine de Richeville. "It aids digestion and tones your stomach to help define your abs."

    Stretch it out: To lengthen and tone limbs, take five minutes to do some simple yoga. Mullins recommends doing downward dog and other stretches in the two weeks leading up to your holiday, and advises repeating the process while you're away if you experience any bloating.

    Hair hydration: Healthy, glossy locks on the beach will make you feel fabulous. Swap your conditioner for a nourishing mask, such as L'Oréal Professionnel Vitamino Color AOX, £14.75, which has a lightweight jelly texture that works on both fine and thick tresses; or for damaged hair, try Pureology Strength Cure Restorative Masque, £26.50.

    Sara Sampaio shot by Emma Tempest for Harper's Bazaar

    One week to go

    Book your wax: Your leg and bikini wax should be left until two or three days before you fly. This gives the skin a chance to settle down before your trip, although it's still essential to cover the area with SPF. To stay hair-free for as long as possible, gently exfoliate with an oil-free formula, such as St Tropez Tan Enhancing Polish, £9.99, twice a week while you're away and follow up with a fragrance-free moisturiser. For those who suffer badly with ingrown hairs, it is worth investing in Decléor Aroma Epil Post Wax Double Action Gel, £21, which soothes the area and slows down hair regrowth.

    Shape and tint: Well-shaped eyebrows give your face a sculpted finish, so book into Blink Brow Bar for threading, or Benefit Cosmetics for waxing the day before your trip. Both offer a brow-tinting service too, so you won't need to worry about shaping or colouring until you get back.

    Finishing touches: To complete your grooming, book in for a manicure and pedicure this week. If your nails are weak and brittle, opt for a shape-and-buff service and avoid coloured polish, as the sun is the best tonic. We recommend the signature manicure at Nails & Brows, £48 for 40 minutes, and for healthy feet, book into Margaret Dabbs for a medical pedicure, £80 for 45 minutes.

    When you arrive: Spritz Nars Body Glow, £44, or Estée Lauder Bronze Goddess Shimmering Body Oil Spray, £35.50, onto your collarbone and other areas of skin that would naturally catch the most sun, to give your limbs subtle definition. "Remember your posture: by rolling your shoulders back and pulling your stomach in, you will look more streamlined," says Martine de Richeville.
