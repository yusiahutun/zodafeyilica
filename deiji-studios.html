    Each month, we look into an exciting and innovative label that is taking the fashion world by storm in our regular feature #TheBrand. This time, we turn our attention to Deiji Studios, an Australian label creating the most beautiful linen and cotton pieces, aiming to blur the lines between what we wear at home, and what we step out of the house in.

    Just last week, Angelina Jolie was photographed at the airport wearing a very luxurious pair of Valentino pyjamas – and she is just the latest in a long line of fashionistas to embrace sleepwear as part of their day-to-day wardrobe.

    This idea, which arguably first belonged to Ukrainian favourite Sleeper, has been picking up steam since we first entered lockdown in 2020. But, where Sleeper is known for fabulous feather-trimmed pyjamas that can also be embraced as partywear, Australian label Deiji Studios is all about bringing that comfort and simplicity from your home life into your day to day.

    Courtesy of Deiji Studios

    Related Story

    “The intention behind Deiji was to create a solution to getting dressed, for the day, night or for bed,” explains Juliette Harkness, who co-founded the brand with Emma Nelson in 2016. “Living on the coast of Australia, we struggled to find clothing that could fit out lifestyles. We needed something you can wear at any hour that is always chic, but most importantly, comfortable.”

    Believing that you are your most confident self when you feel your most comfortable, the founders created a selection of linen sets and robes which are hypoallergenic, breathable, relaxed and perfect for lounging in, but also chic enough to be seen in outside. They have since expanded into oversized poplin dresses, cotton basics and quilted co-ords.

    Courtesy of Deiji Studios

    “We believe in elevated pieces for everyday that can be taken from the home to the outside world with ease,” Nelson tells me. “We think that the future is about making ready-to-wear pieces comfortable, something you can wear out to dinner or to the office, that you are still comfortable in at home. The future of home dressing is versatility.”

    Of course, the brand became extremely popular when the pandemic hit, as we all looked to update and elevate our loungewear wardrobes. But, this is not a trend that they’ve seen be left in 2020. “I don’t think this is something that will get left behind as we move further past the pandemic. Once you go comfortable, you never go back.”

    Courtesy of Deiji Studios

    Related Story

    As well as more time spent inside, the pandemic also gave plenty of us a new outlook on the world, and on our wardrobes.

    “There is constantly a growing focus on the social and environmental impacts of our choices, but the pandemic brought us closer to understanding those impacts,” Harkness says. “When the world stopped, there was a brief peacefulness among our natural environment.”

    “We saw the Venetian canals become clear and host sea life, there were blue skies and air pollution cleared in the cities across China. This gave the world hope, that our choices together can make an impact for the better. I think the pandemic has caused more consumers to buy with intention, longevity and social consciousness.”

    Courtesy of Deiji Studios

    Since the launch six years ago, the founders of Deiji have made a huge effort to be as sustainable as possible, aiming to becoming carbon positive, supporting charitable initiatives local to them and trying to work with the most eco-friendly materials possible, which they list in detail on their website.

    Sustainability is of course a hot topic, but the team stresses how important authenticity is – and they are not afraid to keep moving with the times.

    “Everyone has jumped on the sustainability train, but I think consumers need more than that – they need to feel connected to what they invest in” says Nelson. “Consumers now want to know that their purchases are making real impacts, in the people that they buy from and the planet.”
