    Paraag Marathe has held key roles within the San Francisco 49ers for over 23 years. As President of 49ers Enterprises and Executive VP of Football Ops, he manages money matters and directs football strategies. His expertise lies in negotiating contracts and handling the team’s salary cap, keeping in line with NFL rules.

    Working closely with General Manager John Lynch and Coach Kyle Shanahan, he guides all aspects of the team’s football operations. Marathe’s efforts have been crucial in building and retaining the team’s roster by securing contracts for pivotal players like Arik Armstead, Javon Hargrave, Kyle Juszczyk, George Kittle, Deebo Samuel, Fred Warner, and Trent Williams.

    His astute management ensures the team remains competitive within the salary cap boundaries. Moreover, Marathe co-chairs the NFL’s Future of Football Committee, solidifying his influence in the league.

    Paraag Marathe Early Life, Education & Childhood

    As of 2023, Paraag is approximately 46 years old. Hailing from Saratoga, CA, Marathe’s love for the 49ers began in his early years. A devoted Bay Area sports enthusiast, he hardly missed a 49ers game since age six.

    His strong ties to the Bay Area are evident through his academic achievements, earning a high-honors bachelor’s degree from the Haas School of Business at the University of California-Berkeley and an MBA from Stanford University’s Graduate School of Business.

    Career

    Paraag served in the position of chairman for the USA Cricket. During that time, he made every possible effort so that ICC would recognize the USA for international matches. Eventually, the USA Cricket became the 105th member, which worked together to develop a world-class T20 professional cricket league in the United States.
