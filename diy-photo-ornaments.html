    Homespun Ornament

    BRIAN WOODCOCK

    Celebrate the home where you greet Santa every Christmas with an ornament featuring your humble abode.

    To make: Mod Podge a photograph of your house to an oval wood crafting blank. Once dry, attach a length of felt or twine to the edge with hot glue. Use an awl or small drill bit to create a hole in the top; thread a ribbon through the hole to hang.

    Glistening Snow Ornament

    BRIAN WOODCOCK

    Create a winter wonderland ornament by setting the kids free in a mountain of glittery snow.

    To make: Cut out photographs. Cut out snow and snowflakes from glittery craft paper. Set imagery on one sheet of laminating paper. Top with a second sheet and run through the laminating machine. Cut out ornament to desire shape. Punch a hole in the top; thread a ribbon through the hole to hang.

    In the Doghouse Photo Ornament

    BRIAN WOODCOCK

    Spot has been a good boy this year, so sing his praises by making him his very own ornament.

    To make: Cut a piece of cork to the shape of a small doghouse. Cut out a black piece of craft paper to resemble the doghouse door; glue a picture of the dog to the door. Glue to the cork.

    Trim two tongue depressors to the size needed to create a roof, overlapping trimmed ends at the point. Paint tongue depressor black. Cut snow from white craft paper and glue to roof. Glue mini craft lights to the back of the roof. Glue roof to the top of the cork house.

    Decorate with glitter Santa hat, mini pompom, tinsel trim, and mini craft newspaper marked with the year.

    Laminated Portrait Ornament

    BRIAN WOODCOCK

    Kids grow up so fast, so why not commemorate them year after year with an easy DIY photo ornament? They will love looking at them, and giggling at themselves, when they are all grown up.

    To make: Cut two pieces of decorative craft paper to desired size. Cut a hole (it can be any shape) in one piece of the craft paper that is just smaller than the photo. Tape the photo over the opening. Tape the second piece of paper to the back with double-stick tape (this hides the messy back). Use sticker numbers or cut out numbers from craft paper and glue them to the paper below the picture. Run the ornament through a laminator; round edges. Punch a hole in the top; thread a ribbon through the hole to hang.
