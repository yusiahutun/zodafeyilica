    LYDIA Millen is a very successful YouTube and social media star whose home is just as luxe as her lifestyle.

    The  fashion, fitness, lifestyle and travel influencer began her career in 2012 using Instagram and YouTube to share her favourite outfits, fashion hacks and seasonal trends.

    She also posts clothing hauls, try-on videos and often brings her followers along for a shopping spree.

    And she regularly gives her huge follower base a look at her stunning home.

    Lydia lives in her dream country home in Buckinghamshire with her husband Alistair Keith Gordon.

    It was in October that Lydia took to her popular YouTube channel to give a proper look inside the gorgeous home.

    Read More on Lydia Millen

    The home has its very own social media account where the brunette beauty shares a look inside regularly.

    It boasts a gorgeous kitchen with state-of-the-art appliances and a massive island in the middle, as well as a large pantry.

    As a fan or florals, there’s no shortage of blooms around Lydia’s home and the décor is incredibly chic and characterful.

    The countryside abode features a two large living rooms, one with big cosy sofas in perfect for the star to cuddle up to her dogs.

    There’s a big TV screen and fireplace and a stylish dining room with a chandelier hanging centre stage.

    Another living area has a coffee table, stunning lighting and large doors leading out to the garden.

    Lydia brings a cosy vibe to the abode through the use of candles in all different shapes and sizes.

    Elsewhere, there’s a utility room which has wicker baskets to house products and a white marble sink area.

    The busy YouTube star also has a very organised office with cosy tones, wooden floorboards, a long mirror and a desk to house her computer.

    The bathrooms are just as beautifully decorated as the rest of the space, and there’s a “cloakroom of dreams” too, which features dark interior walls, a mirror and impressive panelling.

    Given that Lydia often impresses fans with her outfits, it’ll come as no surprise that the creator has a dressing room.

    Lydia’s dressing room is reflective of her love of nature, and includes an antique chandelier and pots of thyme which, she says, makes it feel “calm yet full of character.”

    She shared a look back in 2018, and it boasts room a’plenty for her shoes and looks, as well as a large island in the middle to house accessories.

    She’s often said that the biggest project in her home was the hallway, and the married couple have made them bright and as welcoming as possible, with a “New England style to add a grand feel.”

    Lydia is currently looking to buy another home and move from the one she’s currently in, but that hasn’t stopped her and her husband from doing work on the current property.

    She’s put a huge stone area just outside the kitchen which leads down to a big garden.

    Lydia makes no secret of her love for outdoors and she often gives followers a look as she pots plants and sows sweet peas, amongst other nature activities.

    Lydia has a greenhouse where she spends much of her time and she loves to decorate it with antique and rustic décor.

    It was towards the end of last year that Lydia received huge backlash after saying that she had checked into a £1,000 a night hotel because her heating had broken in the midst of the cost-of-living crisis.

     “The heating is currently broken in my house, so I’m heading down to London and am going to check into The Savoy and make full use of their wonderful hot water,” she said in the video.

    TikTok users were quick to fire back on the Savoy remark, as basic rooms at the luxurious hotel can go for around £1,000 per night.

    One person commented: “And here’s me, the heating’s off BECAUSE I CAN’T AFFORD TO PUT IT ON.”

    Millen responded to the comment saying: “My heart breaks too, it’s honestly heartbreaking. I just hope you know other people’s realities can be different and that’s not wrong x.”

    Someone then replied to Millen’s comment saying: “It’s not wrong but it is tone deaf. Heating is a very touchy subject at the minute.”
