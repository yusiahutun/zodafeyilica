    Chris Hemsworth on his physical transformation from Thor to slimmer framed hunk

    He's gone from the buff superhero in Thor to a slimmer stud in flick In The Heart Of The Sea. And even Chris Hemsworth admits there are challenges to having to transform his physique dramatically for different roles.

    26 Jul 2024, 00:00

    He's gone from the buff superhero in Thor to a slimmer stud in flick In The Heart Of The Sea. 

    And even Chris Hemsworth admits there are challenges to having to transform his physique dramatically for different roles.

    Speaking to The Daily Telegraph at the weekend, the 31-year-old Australian actor said: 'I'm not even sure which is my real body anymore'. 

    Scroll down for video  

    Transformation: Chris Hemsworth has opened up about changing his physique for different roles, sporting a slimmer frame last month (left) as opposed to his buff look in 2011 flick Thor (right) 

    'I feel like I've lived like an athlete these last few years because I've constantly had to bulk up for Thor, then lose it all again for something like this', the actor, who is promoting his most recent movie Vacation, added.

    'Getting the weight off is the hardest part,' the former Home And Away star admitted, and it's easy to believe that's not an understatement.

    Chris' steel resolve was put to the test in a different way for his latest role in action flick In The Heart Of The Sea.

    The Australian heartthrob was restricted to a miserly 500 calories a day diet while filming the Ron Howard epic, which sees him take on an 85ft vengeful whale with a single spear.

    Thor what a man! The Byron Bay living star is known for playing thundering demi-God Thor

    'We have to shoot the really skinny stuff where we drop down to 5, 6, 700 calories a day, a good three or four weeks and it's going to be pretty uncomfortable, but we'll be together in our misery,' Chris recently told Entertainment Tonight.

    The average amount of calories recommended for a man per day depends on his size and activity levels, but it's somewhere between 2000-3000.

    People's Sexiest Man Alive 2015 said there was only one thing on his mind during filming.

    'All the bad stuff pizzas, hamburgers, chocolate, ice cream... anything that is not on the menu you have an absolute obsession with,' he reveals in the two minute interview.

    'I spend more time thinking about food than anything else.'

    Suited and booted! Far from his rugged sailor vibes on set the red carpet dressing Chris is much more clean cut, pictured at The Avengers premiere in Hollywood (L) and London (R)

    In December Rush director and Academy Award winner Howard shared a snap of the blonde haired heartthrob on set, revealing his much maligned frame.

    Far from the thundering muscular prowess of Thor the athletic actor looked positively waif-like revealing his slimmed down frame in a blue vest and shirt; the weight fallen from his face.

    In November Hemsworth spoke of his sailing fodder on Jimmy Kimmel Live.

    Far from the Australian diet of barbecues he was raised on the Byron Bay based star revealed, 'It ends up being a couple of small salads and a couple small small pieces of protein.

    'And that's kind of it, and you go to bed hungry. And then you have a fasting period of 15 hours and then you have little meals through the day.'

    Well, hello! The former Home And Away star undergoes a different transformation in new film Vacation

    While he might not have been packing on the blubber for the high concept blockbuster, he was certainly burning calories during filming. 

    Meanwhile Chris also stars in new flick Vacation playing the outrageously flirty and overtly sexual character Stone Crandall in the movie, who hits on his wife Audrey's sister-in-law Debbie Griswold across a number of scenes.

    Stone shows off his buff body in boxer briefs, which also highlights his 'big asset', in one of the now iconic scenes from the film.

    His behaviour sparks a rivalry between Stone and Rusty Griswald, which Chris says makes for some good sparring on-screen.
