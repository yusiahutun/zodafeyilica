    Siegfried Fischbacher - one half of the legendary Las Vegas magic act Siegfried & Roy - had a 'lucky escape' after having emergency life-saving heart surgery in Germany, DailyMailTV can reveal.

    The world famous magician collapsed in pain and was rushed to a hospital while in Munich where he was given the surgery in mid-May.

    Friends say the 79-year-old had a tiny tube inserted into the main artery of his heart after doctors feared he was 'hours away from death or life changing trauma.'

    The friend confirmed that 'it was touch and go', but Siegfried pulled through.

    Siegfried, who turns 80 on June 13,  has since flown back to Las Vegas and is understood to be recuperating at the lavish mansion he shares with business and entertainment partner Roy Horn. 

    Siegfried Fischbacher (pictured in March) collapsed in pain and was rushed to a hospital while in Munich, Germany, where he had life-saving heart surgery in mid May. Friends say the 79-year-old had a tiny tube inserted into the main artery of his heart after doctors feared he was 'hours away from death or life changing trauma'

    One associate revealed the 80s icon, famous as one of Sin City's biggest ever acts, had less than 10 percent of the normal amount of blood entering his heart muscle.

    German-born Siegfried was operated on at a private practice nearby the world famous tourist spot the Hofbrauhaus Tavern.

    The beloved entertainer has now assured friends he 'feels a million percent better and like a teenager.'

    'He is looking forward to celebrating his birthday in style with friends,' one close friend told DailyMail.com.

    The star is already pushing forward with his rehab program doing short walks and breathing exercises.

    Siegfried discovered his health crisis while supporting his long-term partner Roy during Roy's medical trip to Germany.

    Wheelchair-bound Roy, who was famously mauled by one of his tigers on stage in 2003, was undergoing revolutionary stem cell therapy at a special clinic in Munich.

    Siegfried was by Roy's side as he recovered from the treatment, which they hope will eventually help Roy walk again unaided, when Siegfried himself began to feel unwell and couldn't stand properly, suffering throbbing chest pains.

    'Everyone is just so astonished at what happened and how quickly he has bounced back. He was touch and go for a while,' a source close to the famous duo said.

    Siegfried discovered his health crisis while supporting Roy during his own medical trip to Germany.     The friend added: 'He was rushed into surgery and they inserted a stent, which has changed everything. He feels so much better and joked that he feels like a teenager again.

    'Siegfried thanked his medical team for saving his life. He knows he was lucky that it happened while he was in a medical surrounding. 

    'We have been worried for a few months now, as he has been feeling tired, had some odd breathing issues and worked hard to support Roy emotionally.'

    The source explained that Siegfried and Roy were on a personal trip to Bavaria.

    'They love to travel to Germany where Siegfried's sister Margot still lives as a nun at a convent,' the source said.

    'They always visit her in Munich as they are very close friends.

    'This time though they flew over there because Roy wanted to get stem cell therapy.'

    Siegfried details his journey from Rosenheim, Germany to Las Vegas on his website.

    He built his career from a $2 magic book, which caught his eye in 1947, before starting out performing tricks on the TS Bremen liner.  

    After meeting Roy, they formed their animal and magic act and started on boats before moving to the European nightclub circuit.

    Once they incorporated tigers, promoter Tony Azzie asked them to come to Las Vegas in 1967.

    Then in 1981 the Beyond Belief show with Siegfried & Roy at the New Frontier Hotel and Casino started and for more than 20 years, they were kings of the Strip earning tens of millions of dollars.

    But tragedy struck on October 3, 2003, during a show at the Mirage.

    Roy was bitten on the neck and dragged by a seven-year-old male white tiger named Mantacore. The injury to Horn prompted the Mirage to close the show.

    Animal lover Horn insisted the cat 'saved his life' by attempting to drag him to safety after he suffered a stroke and begged for the cat not to be put down. 

    In February 2009, the duo staged a final appearance with Mantacore as a benefit for the Lou Ruvo Brain Institute.

    A year later Siegfried & Roy retired from show business.

    Earlier this year the two appeared together as surprise guests at the Vegas's Keep Memory Alive's 23rd annual Power of Love gala.
