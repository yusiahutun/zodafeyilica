    Hilary Duff WINS $1.25 million lawsuit against female hygiene company... three years after she sued

    Hilary Duff secured a big victory in her lawsuit against female hygiene company Naturalena, following a three-year court battle. TheHow I Met Your Father actress, 36 who is currently expecting her fourth child sued the brand in 2021for failing to pay her endorsement fees.

    05 Jun 2024, 00:00

    Hilary Duff secured a big victory in her lawsuit against female hygiene company Naturalena, following a three-year court battle. 

    The How I Met Your Father actress, 36 — who is currently expecting her fourth child — sued the brand in 2021 for failing to pay her endorsement fees. 

    And on Tuesday it was reported that the founder and CEO of Naturalena, Adrian M. Forsyth, signed a confession of judgment in favor of the Lizzie McGuire star, awarding her $1.25 million in damages. 

    The written agreement willingly accepts the liability and amount of damages agreed upon, as per The Blast.

    A confession of judgement is usually used to avoid a drawn-out legal battle.

    Hilary Duff secured a big  $1.25 million victory in her lawsuit against female hygiene company Naturalena, following a three-year court battle; seen in 2023 at Vanity Fair Oscar Party

    Additionally, Forsyth agreed to cover 'post-judgment interest, Duff’s attorney’s fees, and costs incurred concerning the enforcement of the judgment.'

    The CEO stated that if he were called as a witness in court and placed under oath, he would testify that 'on or about October 1, 2019, Naturalena and the Duff parties entered into a Celebrity Endorsement Agreement.'

    Via this agreement, Duff, through her loan-out company SugarMouse, granted Naturalena exclusive rights to use her image and likeness to promote specific products, such as their baby items. 

    In her lawsuit, Duff accused the company of continuing to use her image and likeness to promote their products despite failing to pay her endorsement fees. 

    Among the items she advertised for the brand were their Veeda feminine hygiene products, Happy Little Campers baby diapers, flushable wipes, and natural cotton wipes. 

    She claimed the company avoided paying her by using an 'unforeseeable circumstances' clause in the contract due to the COVID-19 pandemic. 

    However Duff claimed that it was actually the opposite, and that 'various press reports indicate that Naturalena experienced an increase in demand for its products during the COVID-19 pandemic.'

    'It's nothing more than a bad-faith attempt to exploit the COVID-19 pandemic to evade their clear contractual obligations,' the lawsuit stated.

    Duff had also requested an injunction to ban the company from further use of her publicity rights and asked to be compensated for damages.

    She first shared about the endorsement deal in 2019. 'As parents, we want what’s best for our kids, and as a woman, it’s important to think about my own health when it comes to what I put in my body. Happy Little Camper and Veeda are changing the way we think about the products we use,' she shared.
