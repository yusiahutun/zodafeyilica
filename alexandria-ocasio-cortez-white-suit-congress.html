    Alexandria Ocasio-Cortezs Swearing-In Outfit Was a Nod to Suffragettes and Bronx Girls

    Since becoming the youngest woman ever elected to Congress in November, Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez has been making waves in the political realm. And her official swearing-in ceremony at Capitol Hill yesterday was no exception.

    16 May 2024, 00:00

    Since becoming the youngest woman ever elected to Congress in November, Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez has been making waves in the political realm. And her official swearing-in ceremony at Capitol Hill yesterday was no exception.

    For the momentous occasion, the 29-year-old Bronx native took the opportunity to pay homage to both Suffragettes and fellow Bronx girls with her outfit. For her own sartorial statement, Ocasio-Cortez donned an all-white suit, a nod to the suffragettes who first fought for women's right to vote.

    Getty Images

    The Congresswoman later confirmed the significance of her outfit choice on Twitter. "I wore all-white today to honor the women who paved the path before me, and for all the women yet to come. From suffragettes to Shirley Chisholm, I wouldn’t be here if it wasn’t for the mothers of the movement," she wrote.

    Along with her suffragette white, Ocasio-Cortez also wore her signature red lipstick along with large hoop earrings. Those details were for her fellow Bronx girls—including Sonia Sotomayor, the Supreme Court justice who was once told to wear neutral-colored nail polish for her confirmation hearing.

    "Lip+hoops were inspired by Sonia Sotomayor, who was advised to wear neutral-colored nail polish to her confirmation hearings to avoid scrutiny. She kept her red. Next time someone tells Bronx girls to take off their hoops, they can just say they’re dressing like a Congresswoman," AOC wrote.

    The Bronx and Queens representative's sartorial statement was just one of many made at the 116th Congress swearing-in ceremony yesterday. As a record 127 women were sworn in, many of them used fashion as a medium to celebrate the history-making day.

    Representative Ilhan Omar wore a bright orange and yellow hijab as she became one of the first Muslim women sworn into Congress. Representative Rashida Tlaib wore a traditional Palestinian thobe to honor her mother.

    Representative Deb Haaland, one of the first two Native American women elected to Congress, wore a traditional Pueblo dress, turquoise jewelry, and moccasins.

    Senator Kyrsten Sinema donned an unapolgetically pink (perhaps Elle Woods-inspired) look as she became the first openly bisexual Senator elected to Congress.

    Here's to the new class of Congresswomen getting the job done—and dressing unapologetically badass while doing so.
