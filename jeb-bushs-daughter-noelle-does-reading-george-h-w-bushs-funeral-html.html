    Jeb Bush's daughter Noelle does reading at George H.W. Bush's funeral

    Jeb Bush's daughter Noelle joined three of her cousins for a reading at George H.W. Bush's Texas funeral. Noelle joined the 41st president's fellow granddaughters Barbara Bush, Marshall Bush, and Elizabeth Dwen Andrews to read the Psalm 23.

    13 Jun 2024, 00:00

    Jeb Bush's daughter Noelle joined three of her cousins for a reading at George H.W. Bush's Texas funeral. 

    Noelle joined the 41st president's fellow granddaughters Barbara Bush, Marshall Bush, and Elizabeth Dwen Andrews to read the Psalm 23. 

    Marshall Bush took to the podium first in St. Martin's Episcopal Church in Houston and read from the Old Testament.

    Noelle joined the 41st president's fellow granddaughters Barbara Bush (back center), Marshall Bush (right), and Elizabeth Dwen Andrews (front center) to read the Psalm 23

     Marshall Bush took to the podium first in the St. Martin's Episcopal Church followed by Barbara, who could barely hold back her tears 

    Barbara, who could barely hold back her tears, followed to continue with a reading from Psalm 23.

    Noelle, 41, then went up to recite a short line before Elizabeth finished the reading. 

    While many of Bush's grandchildren have taken public roles in politics, charity, or even Hollywood, Noelle has avoided the spotlight. 

    Jeb Bush's eldest son George, 42, was recently elected to a second term as Texas Land Commissioner and was selected to deliver a eulogy in honor of his grandfather on Thursday. 

    His and Noelle's younger brother Jeb Jr, 34, has also been in the public eye and often appeared in support of his father when Jeb was running for president in 2016.   

     Noelle, 41, then went up to recite a short line before Elizabeth finished the reading

    Noelle didn't join her family on the campaign trail, but she did not go unmentioned. 

    Jeb wrote a candid Medium post in 2016, opening up about Noelle's history with drug abuse and revealing how it would shape his drug policies if he were elected. 

    It was a rare public sighting from Noelle, who had stayed out of the limelight since her drug arrest made headlines in 2002. She is pictured here in her July 2002 mugshot

    'As a father, I have felt the heartbreak of drug abuse,' he wrote. 'My daughter Noelle suffered from addiction, and like many parents facing similar situations, her mom and I struggled to help.' 

    Bush said he 'never expected' to see his 'precious daughter in jail'. 

    'It wasn't easy, and it became very public when I was Governor of Florida, making things even more difficult for Noelle.' 

    'She went through hell, so did her mom [Columba Bush], and so did I.' 

    Noelle was arrested in 2002, when she was 24, on prescription drug fraud. 

    Police were called to a Walgreen's drug store in Tallahassee after Noelle attempted to pick up a prescription for Xanax at the pharmacy drive-through. 

    A Walgreen's pharmacist told police he had received two messages on the store's voicemail from a 'Dr Noelle Scidmore' leaving a detailed Xanax prescription for Noelle Bush. 

    Jeb wrote a candid Medium post in 2016, opening about Noelle's history with drug abuse and revealing how it would shape his drug policies if he were elected. She is pictured here in her January 2002 mugshot

    Noelle later spent 10 days in jail after she was found hiding crack cocaine and pills in her shoe while completing a court-ordered rehabilitation program.  

    'It's very debilitating when you have a loved one who is struggling, and you canâ€™t control it,' Jeb wrote in the Medium post. 

    'You have to love them, but you also have to make it clear you cannot enable the behavior that gets them in trouble.'  

    Jeb also spoke of the day Noelle showed 'a lot of courage' and graduated from a Florida drug court. 

    'I was the proud dad that saw Noelle finish that. She's drug-free now,' he wrote. 

    Noelle's struggles with addiction made headlines when Bush was governor of Florida and her uncle George W Bush was president of the United States. 

    The stories even prompted Jeb to email a Miami Herald reporter in 2003. 

    George H.W Bush is pictured here carrying Noelle, while his wife Babara, son Jeb and grandson George follow close behind in 1980 

    'The only reason you wrote the piece or were told to write the piece is that my struggling daughter is the child of a governor,' he wrote, according to emails obtained by the New York Times. 

    'It won't matter in the whole scheme of things, but I wish the media would leave my daughter alone. It would make it a whole lot easy for her to recover and live a life full of hope and promise.' 

    Fast-forward 15 years later, and Noelle was by her family's side as they put up a united front for their patriarch. 

    Bush's extended state funeral began its final stages on Thursday with the second church memorial in as many days. 

    Jeb Bush was seen holding his wife Columba's hand as they arrived at the church on Thursday

    Noelle's older brother George P Bush delivered a eulogy during the funeral on Thursday 

    Around 1,200 guests filled St Martin's Episcopal Church, including family members, Houston athletes, civic leaders, and Arnold Schwarzenegger. 

    Much as with Wednesday's service in Washington DC, Bush's Texas funeral heavily involved family members as his grandsons served as honorary pallbearers and his granddaughters led various readings.
